				<h3>Welcome Message<br/>
Office of the University President
</h3>
				<p>
			University of Southern Philippines Foundation (USPF) extends its warm welcome to you as it
begins the new Academic Year 2019-2020 and appreciates your interest, either as visitor,
potential student, student, alumna or alumnus, to know more about the University.<br/><br/>
USPF takes pride of its continuing existence and sustaining itself as an educational institution
since 1927 and as a university since 1949 that has produced tens of thousands of responsible
citizens and productive professionals who contributed hugely to their respective communities.<br/><br/>
Per its vision, “USPF is a 21st century transformative learning environment of global lifelong
learners,” with management, staff and faculty who commit themselves to its mission in
providing “a value-laden quality education that nurtures ethical, creative, competent, and social
responsible individuals.”. USPF aims to transform itself “into a dynamic high-tech, high-touch
learning organization in keeping with global standards in education and workplace
requirements.”<br/><br/>
In line with the vision, mission and goal of the institution, USPF has provided and maintained a
campus-wide WIFI connection which expands easy access to internet and web-based on line
resources for use by students faculty and researchers, as well as the university-wide use of its
learning management system (LMS), particularly CANVAS, and which provides an on-line
communication between the university, personnel, faculty, students and their parents. USPF
has achieved to have majority of its curricular programs PACUCOA accredited and is now
preparing for further visits this year and next year to improve further their accreditation levels.
USPF is also preparing for the Institutional Sustainability Assessment (ISA) to be conducted by
CHED in mid July, and the establishment of its Quality Management System (QMS) for better
strategic management and operation of the University, which is an integral part of the
preparations for ISO certification which the current administration will propose to the USPF
Board of Trustees to pursue.<br/><br/>
To prepare for the opening of the new academic year, USPF had embarked on the process of
retooling and sharpening the administrators’ management skills and the faculty’s teaching
competencies and strategies in a three-week in-service training.<br/><br/>
The historic Mabini campus houses the newly renovated Jose Rizal Museum with its original
memorabilia of the national hero which is now in its 2 nd phase, with the upgraded and brighter
lobby and repainted school buildings that pre-school, kindergarten, elementary, junior high
school and senior high school students and alumni can be truly proud of.<br/><br/>
In its more expansive and green Lahug campus, the completion of the new machine shop for
the College of Engineering and Architecture, and the Eriberto S. Jueco Sr. Building that houses
the Senior High School, College of Pharmacy and College of Social Work with a 21 st century

library environment through its Learning Commons as well as science laboratories for physics,
chemistry, pharmaceutics, and biology and microbiology including the research, quality control
and instrumentation rooms, will provide outstanding instruction and outcomes-based student
learning.<br/><br/>
USPF is now offering the recently CHED-approved Expanded Tertiary Education Equivalency and
Accreditation (ETEEAP) in business management courses that offers an opportunity to
individuals to have their extensive commercial, business, management and corporate
knowledge, experience and skills learned from relevant work to be recognized, credited and
given equivalences, and complete the other required subjects, thus obtaining an appropriate
college degree among the various business management programs in a relatively shorter
period. In addition to the current Graduate School programs in Education and Social Work, the
Master of Business Administration (MBA) course will also be offered starting this school year.
Applications for the opening of two new courses in BS Criminology and BS in Library Science
next academic year are now being prepared for submission to CHED.<br/><br/>
In line with its thrust to provide quality education, after one semester of preparation and initial
implementation, USPF will commence this First Semester the full implementation of the
institutional Board and Bar Operations Program that aims to effectively and intensively prepare
the students of courses with government licensure examinations from day one to the last day
of their college studies to pass the board and bar examinations. The complete positive effect of
this program will be felt four years from now all across the courses in Social Work, Nursing,
Education, Engineering, Architecture, Accountancy and Law.<br/><br/>
All of these and other developments, which are proof of our commitment to sustain the legacy
of the USPF Founder, Don Agustin Jereza, will definitely enhance and significantly promote the
improving quality of education the University is giving to its students, providing them
opportunities to do more and be more in everything that they do in a broader and engaging
pedagogical environment while they are in its campuses and when they will face the challenges
of life after leaving the hallowed grounds of USPF.<br/><br/>
Indeed, USPF is simply a great place for students to build and realize their dreams, and prepare
them, having been imbued with the core values of professionalism, integrity, excellence and
social responsibility as well as possessing the essential soft and hard skills, for the challenging
life ahead.<br/><br/>
For USPF and all its stakeholders, may the Academic Year 2019-2020 be abundantly blessed by
the Almighty.       <br/><br/>          
                        
                        
                        
                        </p>

<h2>ATTY. PAULINO A. YABAO</h2>
University President
